Monte Carlo Option Pricing

Quantitative Finance Research Project

Overview

This project implements a Monte Carlo simulation framework to price European call options. It models asset price dynamics using Geometric Brownian Motion and compares numerical results with the Black-Scholes analytical solution to validate accuracy and convergence.

Problem Statement

Financial derivatives are often priced using closed-form solutions, but real-world uncertainty, stochastic behavior, and non-linear dynamics make analytical models insufficient. The challenge was to build a simulation-based pricing engine that captures randomness in asset paths.

Methodology

The model simulates thousands of asset price paths using Geometric Brownian Motion. Each path represents a possible evolution of the underlying asset price over time. Payoffs are calculated at maturity and discounted back to present value.

Mathematical Model

The underlying asset follows:

dS = μS dt + σS dW

Where μ is drift, σ is volatility, and W is a Wiener process.

Results

The Monte Carlo estimator converges toward the Black-Scholes price as the number of simulations increases. This confirms the consistency of stochastic simulation methods in derivative pricing.
