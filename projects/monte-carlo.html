Monte Carlo Option Pricing

Quantitative Research Project

Overview

This project focuses on pricing European options using Monte Carlo simulation. It replicates real-world asset price movements using stochastic processes and compares numerical results with the theoretical Black-Scholes model.

Problem Statement

Financial markets are inherently uncertain and traditional closed-form solutions such as Black-Scholes may not fully capture complex stochastic behavior. The goal was to build a simulation-based pricing engine that can model uncertainty through random sampling and probabilistic paths.

Objective

To estimate European call option prices using Monte Carlo simulation and validate results against analytical Black-Scholes pricing.

Methodology

• Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) was used to simulate asset paths
• Thousands of random price trajectories were generated
• Payoffs were discounted back to present value
• Results were averaged to estimate option price

Results & Outcomes

The simulation converged toward the Black-Scholes price as the number of paths increased. This demonstrated the effectiveness of Monte Carlo methods in financial modelling.
